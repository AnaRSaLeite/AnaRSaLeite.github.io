Ana Rita Sá Leite  

  PhD in Psychology - area of Psycholinguistics
       Georg-August-Universität Göttingen

If you are here, you are probably wondering who I am. You may call me Ana Rita (Ana according to my mom; Rita according to my dad)! I am a researcher devoted to understanding the encoding of syntax during language production and comprehension. My focus is on exploring the potential interplay between the various levels of lexical access to compute each syntactic feature.


Keywords: grammatical gender, gender transparency, bilingualism, lexical access, electroencephalographical measures, eye-tracking.

My current goals

  • Gender processing

    Looking for the neural signatures of gender processing during comprehension using electroencephalography and behavioural measures in multiple language families.

  • Gender transparency

    Understanding the link between word form and gender values across different languages considering a dual-route mechanism and mechanisms of pseudomorphological decomposition.

  • Social bias

    Exploring in a controlled and reliable way whether there is a connection between sex-based stereotypes and the abstract gender of nouns (e.g., woman - skirt) during real-time noun processing.

What is gender, you may ask

It has been described as an unessential and meaningless category with no useful purpose (Ibrahim, 1973; of Ockham, n.d./1980), an unnecessary component of language best to be disposed of (Adelung, 1783), a luxury (Bally, 1935; von der Gabelentz, 1891), a superfluous feature (Fodor, 1959), and even a historical accident, a deformity, a bad habit, the cause for many illnesses, including nightmares, pathological behaviours, erotic and religious delusions, sadism, and savagery (de Courtenay, 1929; Kilarski, 2005, p. 29).

And that's why I study it.
